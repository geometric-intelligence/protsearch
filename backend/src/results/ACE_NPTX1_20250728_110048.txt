**Summary:**

1. **Key Findings Related to ACE and NPTX1:**
   - **ACE (Angiotensin-Converting Enzyme):**
     - ACE inhibitors are commonly prescribed for hypertension management and other chronic conditions like heart failure and chronic kidney disease (PMID: 37284934).
     - ACE inhibitors work by inhibiting the conversion of angiotensin I to angiotensin II, leading to vasodilation and decreased blood pressure (PMID: 37284934).
   - **NPTX1 (Neuronal Pentraxin 1):**
     - NPTX1 is a secreted protein that promotes pancreatic cancer metastatic colonization by acting on the AMIGO2 receptor and enhancing hypoxic growth (PMID: 39028915).
     - Therapeutic targeting of NPTX1 with a monoclonal antibody reduced pancreatic cancer metastasis, suggesting NPTX1 as a potential therapeutic target (PMID: 39028915).
  
2. **Diseases or Conditions Linked to ACE and NPTX1:**
   - **ACE:**
     - ACE inhibitors are used to manage hypertension, heart failure, chronic kidney disease, coronary artery disease, and myocardial infarction (PMID: 28613646).
   - **NPTX1:**
     - NPTX1 is overexpressed in pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma (PDAC) and plays a critical role in promoting PDAC metastatic colonization in the liver (PMID: 39028915).
  
3. **Cellular/Molecular Mechanisms Involving ACE and NPTX1:**
   - **ACE:**
     - ACE inhibitors block the conversion of angiotensin I to angiotensin II, affecting the renin-angiotensin-aldosterone system and leading to vasodilation and reduced blood pressure (PMID: 37284934).
   - **NPTX1:**
     - NPTX1 acts on the AMIGO2 receptor to promote PDAC metastasis by enhancing hypoxic growth and regulating HIF1alpha nuclear retention and function (PMID: 39028915).

4. **Therapeutic Implications:**
   - **ACE:**
     - ACE inhibitors like lisinopril and fosinopril are effective in managing hypertension and reducing cardiovascular strain, with a favorable safety profile (PMID: 29489196, PMID: 32119367, PMID: 37060355).
   - **NPTX1:**
     - Targeting NPTX1 with a monoclonal antibody shows promise in reducing PDAC metastasis, suggesting a potential therapeutic strategy for pancreatic cancer (PMID: 39028915).
